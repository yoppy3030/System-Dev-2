Convenience Stores (Konbini)

7-Eleven, FamilyMart, and Lawson are open 24/7 and offer far more than snacks. You can pay bills, buy concert tickets, send parcels, and even print documents.

Trash Sorting

Sorting garbage is serious in Japan. You must separate waste by burnable, non-burnable, PET bottles, cans, and more.

  • Collection days are strict and vary by neighborhood
  • Labels on trash bags must match local rules
  • Always rinse PET bottles before recycling

Punctuality & Etiquette

Punctuality is deeply valued. Being even a few minutes late is considered rude.

  • Arrive 5 minutes early to appointments or interviews
  • Apologize sincerely if you're late, even by 1 minute

Public Behavior

Quietness is expected in public places. Loud talking on trains or phones is discouraged.

  • Use earphones at low volume
  • Talk quietly, especially on public transport
  • Respect personal space

Biking Rules

Bikes are a common way to get around, but there are strict rules.

  • Use bike lanes where available
  • Do not ride on sidewalks unless allowed
  • Always park in designated bike parking zones

Cash vs. Cards

While cards and apps are growing in use, cash is still king in many parts of Japan.

  • Withdraw cash from 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs
  • Some restaurants and clinics accept only cash

Helpful Mobile Apps

These apps will make life easier:

  • LINE: Most-used messaging app
  • Google Maps / NAVITIME: For transport and directions
  • PayPay / Suica: For easy digital payments
  • Yurekuru: Earthquake alert app
