Professional Life in Japan

Your guide to working and living in Japan as a foreign professional

Business Etiquette

In Japanese workplaces, bowing is a common greeting. Business cards should be given and received with both hands. Punctuality is highly valued.

Work Hours and Holidays

Typical work hours are from 9 AM to 6 PM. National holidays include New Year's, Golden Week, and Obon. Paid leave varies by company.

Visa and Residency

Most foreign workers need a working visa such as Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services. Register at your city office within 14 days of moving to a new residence.

Workplace Communication

Politeness and indirect communication are key. Be prepared to participate in group meetings and company events.

Health Insurance and Pension

Enroll in either national or employee-based health insurance. Pension payments are usually deducted from your salary.

Useful Japanese Phrases

  • おはようございます (Good morning)
  • よろしくお願いします (Nice to meet/work with you)
  • お疲れ様です (Thank you for your hard work)
