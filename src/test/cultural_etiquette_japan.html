Cultural Etiquette in Japan

A guide to navigating social customs and traditions with respect

Bowing Etiquette

Bowing is a core gesture of respect in Japanese culture. The depth and duration depend on the situation—deeper bows for apologies or gratitude, shorter ones for greetings.

Gift Giving

Gift exchange is common in both personal and business settings. It is polite to present a gift with both hands and to modestly decline once before accepting one.

Onsen Manners

Wash your body thoroughly before entering the communal bath. Towels should not touch the water. Tattoos may not be allowed in some onsen, so check in advance.

Shoes Indoors

Remove shoes when entering homes, traditional restaurants, and temples. Use the provided slippers and always place shoes neatly facing the door.

Temple & Shrine Behavior

At shrines, bow once before entering, purify your hands at the chozuya (water basin), and bow twice before praying. Be quiet and respectful in these sacred places.

Table Manners

Do not stick chopsticks upright in rice. Say "itadakimasu" before eating and "gochisousama deshita" after finishing. Don’t point or play with chopsticks.
